## üìã Your Legal Responsibilities

### 1. Age Verification of ALL Performers

**Federal Law: 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 2257**

You MUST:
- ‚úÖ Verify age of every person in your content
- ‚úÖ Maintain copies of government-issued IDs
- ‚úÖ Keep records for 7 years minimum
- ‚úÖ Update records when performers appear in new content

**"Performer" includes:**
- Anyone appearing in sexual content
- Anyone nude or partially nude
- Anyone in sexually suggestive situations
- Even non-sexual performers if in adult content context

**Penalties for non-compliance:**
- Up to 5 years imprisonment (first offense)
- Up to 10 years imprisonment (repeat offense)
- Fines up to $100,000
- Criminal record

#### How to Verify Performers:

**Solo Creators (you're the only performer):**
1. Complete age verification through Fanz
2. Your verification covers your solo content
3. No additional records needed for solo work

**Collaborations (multiple performers):**
1. **Before filming:**
   - Obtain government-issued ID from each performer
   - Photocopy ID (front and back)
   - Have performer sign "2257 Model Release Form"
2. **Store records:**
   - Keep physical or digital copies (encrypted)
   - Store for 7 years minimum
   - Make available for inspection by FBI
3. **Upload to Fanz:**
   - Before posting content with multiple performers
   - Upload ID copies + model release through Creator Portal
   - Fanz verifies and maintains secondary records

**Model Release Form must include:**
- Performer's legal name (as shown on ID)
- Stage names/aliases used
- Date of birth
- ID type and number
- Date content was produced
- Description of content
- Signature and date

### 4. Maintain 2257 Records

**Federal Law: 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 2257**

You MUST maintain these records for each performer:

**Primary Producer Records (Required):**
1. Legal name (as shown on government ID)
2. Date of birth
3. Copy of government-issued ID
4. Any aliases or stage names used
5. Date content was produced
6. Content description and location (URLs)

**Secondary Producer Records (Maintained by Fanz):**
- Fanz maintains duplicate records as "Secondary Producer"
- You remain "Primary Producer" with full responsibility
- Fanz records don't absolve you of responsibility

**Record Retention:**
- **7 years minimum** from last content publication
- Cannot be deleted even if performer requests
- Must be available for FBI inspection within 48 hours

**Storage Requirements:**
- Physical location must be listed on content (Custodian of Records)
- Must be accessible during business hours
- Can be inspected by FBI without warrant
- Recommend encrypted digital storage + physical backup

### 5. Report Trafficking Indicators

**Federal Law: FOSTA-SESTA**

If you encounter content or performers showing trafficking signs, you MUST report:

**13 Trafficking Indicators:**
1. Third-party management/control
2. Coerced performance
3. Inability to keep earnings
4. Restricted communication
5. Multiple performers in single account
6. Rapid location changes
7. Hotel/motel backgrounds (transient)
8. Age ambiguity
9. Distress signals
10. Substance abuse indicators
11. Branding/tattoos (ownership marks)
12. Inconsistent stories
13. Fear of authorities

**If you see any of these:**
1. Do NOT collaborate with that performer
2. Report to Fanz: trafficking@fanz.com
3. If immediate danger: Call 888-373-7888 (National Human Trafficking Hotline)

**Failure to report trafficking can result in criminal charges under FOSTA-SESTA.**

### Q: Do I need to show my face?
**A:** No. Many successful creators don't show their faces. Options:
- Mask/blindfold
- Frame shots below neck
- Focus on specific body parts
- Use creative angles
- Consider your goals (anonymous vs. brand building)

---

### Q: Can I have a "vanilla" job and be a creator?
**A:** Yes, but consider:
- ‚úÖ Legal: Not illegal to be a creator
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Employment risk: Some employers have morality clauses
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Privacy risk: Coworkers may discover your content
- ‚úÖ Financial: Extra income stream

**Recommendations:**
- Use stage name
- Keep separate email/phone
- Don't mention workplace in content
- Be prepared for potential discovery

---

### Q: What if someone I know finds my content?
**A:** This is a risk all creators face. Consider:
- **Prevention:** Use stage name, don't show face, geofence content
- **If discovered:** You're doing legal work. You don't owe anyone an explanation.
- **Support:** Consider therapy to process feelings about discovery
- **Boundaries:** You don't have to discuss your work if you don't want to

---

### Q: Can I create content with my partner?
**A:** Yes, but:
- ‚úÖ Both partners must verify age
- ‚úÖ Both must complete 2257 records
- ‚úÖ Both must consent to content distribution
- ‚úÖ Consider joint account vs. separate accounts
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Have "what if we break up" conversation beforehand

### Q: What equipment do I need?
**Minimum:**
- Smartphone with good camera (iPhone 12+, recent Android)
- Good lighting (ring light)
- Tripod or phone mount

**Recommended:**
- DSLR or mirrorless camera
- Multiple light sources (key light, fill light, back light)

### Q: How do I handle negative comments?
**A:** Expect negativity. Strategies:

- **Block aggressively** - No second chances
- **Disable comments** - If needed for mental health
- **Report harassment** - Use platform reporting tools
- **Focus on positive fans** - They outnumber haters

**Remember:** Negative comments say more about the commenter than you.
